HTML Text Formatting.

  1. bold (b)
    • This tag is more straightforward and simply makes the text inside it bold visually.
    • Do not use for entire paragraphs or long blocks of text
    • Avoid overusing, as it can make text difficult to read
  2. strong
    • This tag is used to indicate strong importance or emphasis. By default, browsers render text inside strong tags as bold.
    • Can be used to highlight key points, warnings, or notices
    • Can be combined with other formatting tags, such as bold or italic, to create emphasis
  3. emphasis (em)
    • Similar to italic, use to indicate emphasis or to differentiate between words or phrases
    • Can be used for book or movie titles, foreign words or phrases, or to denote thoughts or quotes within a sentence
    • Can also be used to indicate a change in tone or mood
  4. italic (i)
    • Use to indicate emphasis or to differentiate between words or phrases
    • Can be used for book or movie titles, foreign words or phrases, or to denote thoughts or quotes within a sentence
    • Avoid overusing, as it can become distracting or lose its impact
  5. underline (u)
    • The "u" tag is used to underline text within a web page.
    • Avoid using for entire blocks of text, as it can make it difficult to read
    • It's a straightforward way to visually emphasize text by placing a line underneath it.
